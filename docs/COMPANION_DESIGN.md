# BITLING Living Companion Design

## Product promise

BITLING should feel persistent, observant and capable of change. It must not pretend to be conscious or manipulate the player into returning. Its sense of life comes from consistent simulation, memory, authored reactions and visible consequences.

## Core loop

1. **Observe** — the player reads BITLING's mood, intention and needs.
2. **Choose** — care, play, learn, rest, explore or solve a challenge.
3. **React** — animation, language and state change immediately acknowledge the action.
4. **Remember** — meaningful events influence familiarity, trust and personality.
5. **Develop** — skills, appearance, dialogue and available activities branch over time.
6. **Reflect** — learning challenges connect the game event to a transferable mental skill.

## Sources of depth

### Companion state

- physical needs;
- mood;
- relationship and trust;
- personality traits;
- current autonomous intention;
- short interaction history;
- long-term meaningful memories.

### Player mastery

- pattern recognition;
- working memory;
- planning;
- language;
- programming concepts;
- creativity;
- emotional reflection;
- strategic decision-making.

### Evolution

Evolution requirements should combine:

- level range;
- dominant personality traits;
- relationship quality;
- completed learning paths;
- important decisions;
- discovered memories;
- optional secrets.

No evolution path should be permanently ruined by taking a break from the game.

## Inspiration boundaries

BITLING may learn from the design strengths of creature raising, collection RPGs, virtual pets and learning games, but it must not reproduce protected characters, terminology, visual silhouettes, maps, dialogue or proprietary battle systems. Every mechanic needs a distinct BITLING rationale and presentation.

## Engagement standard

The target is durable intrinsic engagement:

- curiosity rather than fear of loss;
- meaningful progress rather than infinite chores;
- self-expression rather than social pressure;
- competence rather than artificial friction;
- attachment through continuity rather than guilt.

## Vertical-slice acceptance criteria

The first playable slice is successful when a new player can:

- meet and hatch BITLING;
- understand its current state without a tutorial wall;
- perform at least four meaningful interactions;
- see relationship and personality change;
- complete one learning activity;
- close and reopen the game without losing state;
- play comfortably on iPhone, iPad and desktop layouts.
