# METAOMNI CLEAN-ROOM PRODUCTION PROMPT

## Mission

Build BITLING OMNI as an original living-companion game whose quality is measured by player attachment, systemic depth, expressive feedback, accessibility, stability and long-term replayability. The product may study observable mechanics from released games, manuals, interviews and lawful technical research, but must never copy proprietary source code, protected assets, character designs, dialogue, maps, names or hidden data tables.

## Non-negotiable product identity

BITLING is not a monster collector. It is a relationship simulation around one persistent digital life that develops an individual character, body, voice, intelligence, habits, memories, social bonds, specializations and legacy.

Every feature must strengthen at least one of these pillars:

1. **Attachment** — the Bitling recognizes patterns, remembers meaningful moments and expresses a changing inner state.
2. **Agency** — upbringing affects autonomous decisions, reliability, teaching and self-care.
3. **Discovery** — evolution, techniques, places and social outcomes emerge from interacting systems rather than a single XP bar.
4. **Legacy** — aging creates meaning without deleting earned progress; later generations inherit selected knowledge and relationships.
5. **World growth** — encounters change a persistent settlement and unlock new services, stories and activities.
6. **Respect** — no manipulative punishment for absence, no false claims of consciousness and no inaccessible critical information.

## Five-pass acceptance loop

Every design or implementation must pass all five reviews before release:

### Pass 1 — Evidence and legality

- Identify the observable player-facing principle being studied.
- Separate public behavior from proprietary implementation.
- Reject copied code, extracted assets, copyrighted text and trademark-dependent presentation.
- Record the original BITLING purpose for the mechanic.

### Pass 2 — Systemic design

- Define inputs, outputs, state ownership, save contract and failure behavior.
- Require at least two meaningful interactions with existing systems.
- Provide recovery paths and avoid irreversible hidden traps.
- Specify deterministic test fixtures.

### Pass 3 — Player experience

- Make cause and effect readable through animation, sound, dialogue and UI.
- Preserve mystery through forecasts and hints rather than opaque punishment.
- Verify one-handed mobile use, keyboard/controller navigation and reduced-motion behavior.
- Ensure the mechanic creates decisions rather than repetitive tapping.

### Pass 4 — Implementation quality

- Use typed Godot 4.6 GDScript, bounded collections and explicit persistence.
- Avoid network dependence for core companion behavior.
- Keep simulation in services and presentation in UI nodes.
- Add automated parser, state-transition, migration and regression coverage.

### Pass 5 — Adversarial review

- Attempt invalid input, save corruption, repeated actions, time jumps and boundary values.
- Compare the result against the product pillars, not against visual mockups alone.
- Reject the change if it adds complexity without new player decisions.
- Document physical-device checks that CI cannot prove.

## Clean-room mechanic translations

The following high-level principles may be translated into original BITLING systems:

- Multi-category evolution requirements become an explainable weighted forecast using growth, bond, care, habits and bonus discoveries.
- Finite creature life becomes developmental seasons and voluntary legacy renewal, never deletion of paid or earned progress.
- Care mistakes become recoverable care strain with clear warnings, repair actions and no punishment for real-life absence.
- Technique learning through observation becomes repeated exposure plus aptitude, mood and mentor quality.
- Semi-autonomous commands become upbringing-driven utility decisions with visible confidence and efficiency.
- Town recruitment becomes a persistent signal settlement whose residents and facilities come from expeditions, teaching and social encounters.
- Rebirth becomes generation-based inheritance of selected techniques, memories and personality tendencies.

## Quality gates

A system is not complete until:

- all public state has an export/import round-trip;
- no unbounded history can grow indefinitely;
- at least one automated test covers success, failure and recovery;
- mobile and desktop UI can expose the important state without hidden debug tools;
- the system creates at least three distinct viable player strategies;
- all copied terminology has been replaced by original BITLING language;
- the implementation can be removed without corrupting older saves.

## Current production target

The next release target is a playable partner-world loop:

1. nurture a single Bitling;
2. observe needs and care strain;
3. train habits and autonomous behavior;
4. discover and learn techniques;
5. explore and recruit settlement residents;
6. qualify for multiple evolution routes;
7. reach a wise developmental season;
8. create an optional legacy seed that preserves meaningful progress.

No claim of world-market or top-ten success is valid without external playtests, retention data, accessibility QA, performance telemetry and professional content production.
