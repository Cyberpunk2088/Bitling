# Habitat World Consequence Contract

BITLING's relationship systems are invalid if their consequences remain confined to meters, dialogue text or save data. Formed habits and unresolved conflict must alter the shared habitat and return as playable situations.

## Non-negotiable rules

1. Every one of the fifteen habitat approaches has an explicit room manifestation.
2. A formed habit creates exactly one initial manifestation and one follow-up initiative.
3. Persistent marks remain attached to canonical habitat hotspots.
4. Follow-up initiatives and conflict events become the active habitat moment until addressed.
5. Resolving a follow-up must reinforce, transform or redirect the room state.
6. Conflict resolution must mechanically reduce persistent friction; it cannot be cosmetic text.
7. World marks, pending events and resolved history use the existing atomic habitat save path.
8. World events preserve open-ended agency and never prescribe a correct lens.
9. Persistent visuals render inside the production 3D stage and never intercept input.
10. Every descendant layer must continue satisfying the habitat and behavior gates.

## Consequence lifecycle

```text
multi-session habit or conflict threshold
→ persistent hotspot manifestation
→ Xogot initiative or conflict follow-up
→ player chooses an approach
→ room state is reinforced, transformed or redirected
→ result remains visible and persisted
```

The architecture gate, deliberate mutation suite and Godot runtime suite are the executable form of this contract.
