# Habitat Behavior Contract

BITLING's habitat decisions must create persistent, legible behavioral consequences without turning Xogot into a random obstruction or a programmable puppet.

## Non-negotiable rules

1. Habits require at least three distinct sessions and two distinct contexts.
2. Repeating a choice inside one session cannot strengthen the habit twice.
3. Every choice exposes Xogot's anticipated response before commitment.
4. Conflict is deterministic and derived from prior orientation plus unresolved friction.
5. Resistance changes XP and non-essential effects; essential care remains available.
6. Formed habits change `CompanionBrain.current_intention` and therefore future habitat moments.
7. Habits, orientations and conflicts use the existing atomic habitat save path.
8. No recommendation, hidden answer key or random refusal may be introduced.

## Execution states

- **OFFEN / AUFGEGRIFFEN**: Xogot executes the intention with full effectiveness.
- **AUSHANDELN / VERÄNDERT**: Xogot keeps the intention but modifies execution.
- **GRENZE**: Xogot performs only the necessary care component and rejects the rest.
- **GEWOHNHEIT**: a multi-session, multi-context pattern is influencing future intention.

The architecture gate, mutation suite and Godot runtime test are the executable form of this contract.
